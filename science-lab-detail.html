Characteristics

Similar to NASA astronauts and engineers, lab scientists collect materials and tools in order to carry out experiments. Lab scientists generally design their own experiments and determine in advance the tools and materials needed to perform the procedures. Furthermore, experiments range in length, so lab scientists often multitask by performing multiple experiments at the same time. In doing so, they must keep track of procedure steps, measurements, and time for each simultaneous experiment. Lastly, many scientists share common laboratory space, so communal tools and materials must be organized and their statuses, communicated.

Observations

We observed 7 biology and chemistry scientists in laboratories at University of Pittsburgh and Carnegie Mellon University for a period of 1.5 to 2 hours each.
